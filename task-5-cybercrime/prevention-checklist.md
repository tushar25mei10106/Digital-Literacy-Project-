# Prevention Checklist (for College Students in India)



1. &#x20;Use strong, unique passwords
  Create different passwords for each account (email, social media, banking). Use a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols.
   ---
2. &#x20;Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
  Turn on 2FA for important accounts like email, Instagram, and banking apps to add an extra layer of security.
   ---
3. &#x20;Beware of phishing links
  Don’t click on suspicious links in emails, WhatsApp, or SMS—especially those asking for login details or urgent action.
   ---
4. &#x20;Never share OTPs or passwords
  No bank, company, or genuine service will ever ask for your OTP or password.
   ---
5. &#x20;UPI Safety Tip #1: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money
  Entering your UPI PIN always means you are sending money, not receiving it.
   ---
6. &#x20;UPI Safety Tip #2: Avoid unknown QR codes and collect requests
  Don’t scan random QR codes or approve payment requests from unknown people.
   ---
7. &#x20;Use secure Wi-Fi networks
  Avoid logging into sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi (like in cafes or college campuses). Use mobile data or a trusted network.
   ---
8. &#x20;Keep your devices updated
  Regularly update your phone, laptop, and apps to fix security vulnerabilities.
   ---
9. &#x20;Limit personal information online
  Don’t overshare details like phone number, address, or college ID on social media.
   ---
10. ## Install apps only from trusted sources

## &#x20;    Download apps only from Google Play Store or Apple App Store and check reviews before installing.
