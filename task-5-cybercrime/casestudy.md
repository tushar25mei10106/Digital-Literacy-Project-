### Case Study: **UPI / Online Payment Fraud**

#### UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. It usually happens through social engineering rather than technical hacking.

#### 

#### A typical scenario begins when the fraudster contacts the victim via call, SMS, or messaging apps, pretending to be a bank official, customer support agent, or even a buyer/seller. Step 1: The attacker gains trust by offering help (e.g., resolving an issue, sending a refund, or confirming a payment). Step 2: They send a “collect request” or ask the victim to scan a QR code. Step 3: The victim is instructed to enter their UPI PIN, believing they are receiving money. Step 4: Instead, the entered PIN authorizes a payment, and money is instantly transferred to the fraudster’s account.

#### 

#### Victims are often students, elderly individuals, small business owners, or anyone unfamiliar with how UPI works. People who urgently expect payments or refunds are especially vulnerable.

#### 

#### The consequences can be severe. Victims may lose significant amounts of money within minutes, and recovery is difficult due to the instant nature of UPI transactions. Additionally, there is emotional stress, loss of trust in digital systems, and sometimes reputational damage if business accounts are affected.

#### 

#### This case highlights the importance of awareness: never share your UPI PIN, and remember—entering a PIN always means you are sending money, not receiving it.
